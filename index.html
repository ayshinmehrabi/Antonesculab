About Our Lab

Quantitative Cell Biology at the Nanoscale

Welcome to the [Lab Name] at Toronto Metropolitan University. Our research focuses on understanding how cells organize their membranes, communicate through receptors, and regulate organelle function in health and disease.

We combine advanced fluorescence microscopy, biochemistry, and cell biology to investigate fundamental questions in membrane biology — and to develop new tools and therapeutic strategies.

We are a diverse, collaborative group committed to rigorous science and the mentorship of trainees at all levels.

Research Areas

What We Study

Our lab investigates membrane biology and cell signaling through six interconnected research themes, combining quantitative microscopy, biochemistry, and chemical biology.

📡

Nanoscale Organization of Receptor Signaling

How are signaling receptors organized at the nanoscale on the cell surface, and how does this spatial organization regulate signal output?

Learn more →
⚡

Cell Metabolism and Organelle Dynamics

How do metabolic states influence organelle morphology, positioning, and function? We investigate mitochondria, lysosomes, and ER remodeling.

Learn more →
💊

Therapy and Targeted Drug Delivery Development

Leveraging our understanding of membrane trafficking to design targeted drug delivery systems and identify novel therapeutic strategies.

Learn more →
🔄

Clathrin Mediated Endocytosis

Dissecting the molecular machinery that drives clathrin-coated pit nucleation, maturation, and scission at the plasma membrane.

Learn more →
🧪

Phosphoinositides and Lipid Signalling

How do phosphoinositide lipids orchestrate membrane identity, vesicle trafficking, and signal transduction across cellular compartments?

Learn more →
🔭

Developing New Tools for Microscopy

Engineering fluorescent probes, biosensors, and imaging approaches to push the boundaries of what can be observed in living cells.

Learn more →

Our Commitments

Code of Conduct

We are committed to creating a respectful, inclusive, and scientifically rigorous environment where every member can thrive.

01

Respect & Inclusion

We treat all members with dignity regardless of background, identity, or experience level. Discrimination, harassment, and exclusion are not tolerated.

02

Scientific Integrity

We conduct and report research honestly and transparently. Fabrication, falsification, and plagiarism violate our shared values and institutional policy.

03

Open Collaboration

We share knowledge generously, give credit fairly, and communicate openly. We value collaboration over competition.

04

Wellbeing & Balance

We respect that lab members are whole people. We support mental health, reasonable working hours, and time outside the lab.

05

Mentorship & Growth

We invest in each member's scientific and personal development. Constructive feedback and career guidance are part of everyday lab culture.

06

Reporting & Accountability

Anyone who experiences or witnesses misconduct is encouraged to speak with the PI or use institutional reporting channels.

Questions or Concerns?

If you have questions about our code of conduct or wish to raise a concern, please reach out to Dr. Lastname directly or contact the TMU Graduate Coordinator. You may also use TMU's formal complaint procedures.
